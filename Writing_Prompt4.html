I long for a lack of technology, like early 2000s was my favorite. Everyone watched the shows that came out on Wednesdays and Friday nights. There were shows that were less bingey, everyone had phones but not so advanced that everyone was on them all the time. And I think what I really miss is how things felt a little more… earned. Like you couldn’t just have everything whenever you wanted, so when you did get it, it actually meant something. Waiting all week for a new episode, talking about it the next day, making plans around it. It made time feel structured in a way that felt comforting, like there were small things to look forward to instead of everything just blending together. People also just felt more there. Like if you were hanging out with someone, you actually had their full attention. Not in a forced way, just because there wasn’t anything else pulling them away every two seconds. Conversations felt slower, a little more real, less interrupted. Even silence didn’t feel awkward in the same way because you weren’t constantly trying to fill it. I also feel like boredom used to be more normal, and weirdly more important. You’d sit around with nothing to do and eventually you’d find something, or you’d think, or you’d talk. Now it’s like the second there’s any gap, it gets filled instantly. There’s no space to just exist for a second without something coming at you. And there was something nice about everyone kind of being on the same page. Watching the same things, hearing the same music, sharing the same references without trying. It made connection feel easier, like you didn’t have to search for it. I don’t even think it’s really about the technology itself, it’s more about the pace it created. Things moved slower, and because of that, they felt a little more meaningful. I think there’s something in that about how to live now, like maybe not everything needs to be instant. Maybe it’s okay to wait, to miss something, to not be constantly updated. Maybe the point isn’t to have access to everything, but to actually feel the things you do have. I think I just miss how natural it all felt. Like people weren’t trying so hard to keep up with everything all the time. You could just exist in your day, look forward to small things, and not feel like you were missing something every second you weren’t online.
