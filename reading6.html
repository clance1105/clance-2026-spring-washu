This reading made me think less about what technology does and more about how it quietly shapes the way I exist day to day. I don’t usually question the systems I’m part of, I just use them, but they’re constantly guiding my behavior, what I notice, and even what I value. Like, certain tools make things feel more efficient, but they also make me expect everything to be instant, which changes my patience and attention without me realizing it. It also got me thinking about how uneven the impact of technology can be. I might experience something as convenient or helpful, while someone else is affected by it in a way they never chose. That imbalance is easy to ignore when you’re the one benefiting from it. And a lot of the time, the effects aren’t obvious, they’re subtle, built into everyday habits, so they don’t feel like decisions even though they are. I guess what stuck with me is that using technology isn’t really a neutral act, even if it feels like one. It’s more like participating in a system that has consequences beyond what I see directly. That makes me want to be a bit more aware of what I’m engaging with and why, instead of just assuming it’s all harmless or inevitable.
