I’ve basically grown up with technology being a part of me. By high school I was already tracking myself, first with an applewatch, and then an iPhone. It wasn’t a conscious decision; it just felt normal to layer technology onto my body and let it quietly shape how I moved through the day. Looking back, thinking of that as a kind of “cyborg” state changes how I read it. It’s not dramatic or sci-fi, it’s subtle. You start outsourcing awareness for certain stuff in your life. How many steps? How much sleep? Do I have any texts or notifications? And it happens so gradually that you rarely stop to question how much your body and the network are intertwined. I think it's probably time I try to distance myself from screens. It's just so hard. It’s strange that I’ve been comfortable identifying with something as abstract as being “part machine,” but hadn’t really considered how much the spaces around me change and reflect that experience. The cyborg idea feels distant, almost conceptual when in fact it's real and present.
