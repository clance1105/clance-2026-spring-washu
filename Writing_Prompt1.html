I think watching tv or a show before bed is probably the most important ritual I have. I know it sounds silly, how could that be true?! And yes I know its bad for me, but something about it, even after the most stressful of days winding down to watching something I love or watching something that distracts me from real life is important to be because it gives me a way to shut my brain off without feeling like I’m just lying there stuck in my thoughts. It's like a buffer between the chaos of the day and actually trying to sleep. Even if it’s not the “healthiest” habit, it’s consistent, and that matters to me. It’s something I can rely on, same routine, same feeling of comfort, and it helps me reset a little. I think more than anything, it just makes the day feel finished, like I can let go of everything that happened and not carry it into tomorrow. I think without it I would feel that real life is all consuming and like I have to be “on” all the time. Sometimes I just want to turn it off and tune out the world. I experience a lot of emotions throughout the day, joy, sadness, fear, anxiety, etc. This is really the only time where those feelings would be about something inconsequential to real life, take last night for example. I watched my favorite character die and I was upset and sad, but then realized how that was make-believe and not important or had any impact on my daily life. As humans I believe we go through so much in a single day, way more than we’re meant to just carry nonstop without any kind of release. I think we need small, intentional moments where we can step outside of our own lives and feel things safely, without consequences. Watching something before bed kind of does that for me. It lets me process emotions in a way that feels contained, like I can feel sadness or excitement or even stress, but it’s not mine to hold onto after. It almost feels like a reset for my emotions, like I can let everything from the day settle and then replace it with something lighter or at least something separate from me. And I think that matters more than whether it’s “good” or “bad” for sleep. Because at the end of the day, being able to turn things off, even just a little, is what actually lets me rest.
