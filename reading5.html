Honestly this reading made me realize how used I am to constantly being pulled by technology. I pretty much always have something on me my phone, my laptop and I don’t even question how often notifications and messages interrupt me. The idea of “calm technology” felt kind of refreshing because it suggests tech doesn’t always have to demand my full attention. I liked the idea of it sitting more in the background and only coming forward when I actually need it. That’s so different from what I’m used to, where everything is trying to grab me instantly. Even thinking about stuff like smart fridges I used to think those were so cool, but now it just sounds like another screen I don’t need. It made me realize that more tech isn’t always better, especially if it just adds more noise. Overall it made me think that the real goal shouldn’t just be making new technology, but designing it in a way that actually respects my attention and doesn’t overwhelm me.
