About

Invisible Cities imagines cities less as fixed places and more as fragments of memory, symbols, desires, and contradictions. Each city becomes an idea as much as a location.

This concept turns the city into a clickable archive. The map does not behave like a literal diagram. Instead, it acts more like a field of associations: each marker is an entry point into one urban condition, one story, one atmosphere.

On this page, the about text and map sit together in one continuous scroll, so reading and wandering happen in the same space. Hovering over a marker reveals a title and short summary below, and clicking opens the full city page.

You can duplicate the marker structure to add more cities later, each with its own title, summary, image treatment, and descriptive text.

Zaira
Memory / Trace / Measure

Zaira does not disclose its past directly. Instead, the city holds its history in alignments, distances, scars, repetitions, and the physical residue of lives once lived. Its meaning comes from relations rather than isolated objects.

In this web version, the city page acts like an expanded reading condition. The red title bar isolates the city as a chapter, while the pale field becomes a space for an image, drawing, collage, or ornamental graphic.
